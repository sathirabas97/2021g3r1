Apartment

A large building that is divided into rooms for living in.

Commuter

: A person who regularly travels quite a far way between work and home

Nightclub

Feeling or showing an envious resentment of someone or their achievements, possessions, or perceived advantages.

Pawnshop

A shop where people can leave a possession in order to get money

Poverty

The situation of having little money and few possessions.

Sidewalk

A path with a hard surface on the side of a road (American English, same as pavement)

Skyscraper

A very tall modern building

Street

A public road in a city or town that has houses or shops on both sides

Suburb

An area where people live which is located on the edge of a large city

Town

A place where people live and work, usually smaller than a city

Traffic

a lot of vehicles moving along a road

Slum

An area, often illegal, with no facilities where very poor people live.

Pavement

A path with a hard surface on the side of a road (British English, same as sidewalk).

Crowded

Feeling of doing an action/statement causing or intended to cause harm or trouble

Drugstore

A shop where people can buy medicines, beauty products, etc

Freeway

A wide road where vehicles travel fast for long distance (American English, same as motorway).

Monument

A building or statue that is built to honor a famous person or event

Luxurious

To be very comfortable and expensive

Downtown

The central part of a city

Outskirts

The areas that form the edge of cities and towns
